About your stay

Villa Sinodium offers comfortable self-catering accommodation in a quiet countryside setting, within easy reach of the Adriatic coast and nearby towns. The property is surrounded by open landscape and typical Dalmatian scenery, making it a calm place to unwind after a day at the beach or out exploring.

The layout works well for couples and families who want space to relax together, with practical amenities for cooking, dining, and time outdoors—including a private pool and terrace for sunny afternoons and evenings.

From here you can plan straightforward day trips to Krka National Park, the historic city of Šibenik, the Zrmanja River and its canyon scenery, and the vibrant waterfront city of Split, then return to a relaxed, rural pace in the evenings.

Whether you are focused on the sea, nature walks, or simply enjoying the villa, Villa Sinodium is intended as a welcoming, low-key base for a restorative stay in central Dalmatia.

The name Sinodium

The name Sinodium originates from an ancient Roman settlement that once existed in this region. Historical sources mention that the settlement of Sinodium was destroyed during Roman military campaigns led by Octavian in the 1st century BC, during conflicts with the Illyrian tribe of the Delmatae. This historical reference highlights the deep cultural and historical roots of the area and connects the modern location to its Roman-era heritage.

Things to visit

Day-trip ideas within reach of the villa.

Krka National Park

Krka is known for its stepped waterfalls, river scenery, and walking trails through forest and rock. Skradinski Buk is the park’s famous cascade area—ideal for a relaxed nature day.

The park is an easy drive from the Šibenik hinterland, making it a practical outing when you want shade, freshwater swimming where permitted, and dramatic views without a long journey.

Šibenik

Šibenik combines a UNESCO-listed historic core with a lively Mediterranean waterfront. Narrow stone streets lead to viewpoints over the bay, cafés, and small local shops.

St James’ Cathedral is a highlight of the old town—an impressive Renaissance landmark that anchors the city’s heritage charm.

Zrmanja River

Discover the breathtaking beauty of the Zrmanja River, one of Croatia’s most picturesque natural treasures. Flowing through dramatic limestone canyons and surrounded by untouched wilderness, the river is famous for its crystal-clear waters and peaceful atmosphere.

Whether you choose kayaking, rafting, or simply visiting one of the scenic viewpoints, Zrmanja offers unforgettable views and a true escape into nature. It’s an ideal destination for adventure lovers, photographers, and anyone looking to experience the wild beauty of inland Dalmatia.

Split

Split centres on Diocletian’s Palace, where Roman walls enclose lanes filled with restaurants, boutiques, and street life. It is one of Croatia’s best introductions to layered history beside the sea.

Beyond the palace, you will find city beaches, ferries to the islands, shopping, and a lively evening atmosphere—perfect for a full-day urban contrast to the countryside calm near the villa.
